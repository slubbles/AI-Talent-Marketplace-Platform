# Phase 2 and Phase 3 Roadmap

The MVP deliberately stops short of several expansion areas. This roadmap keeps those decisions explicit.

## Phase 2

Target:
- deepen operational workflows and marketplace trust without changing the platform core

### Candidate features

| Feature | Why it matters | Estimated effort |
|------|------|------|
| Real LinkedIn integration | profile enrichment and recruiter sourcing depth | 1 to 2 weeks |
| Stripe billing and invoicing | monetization and fee collection | 2 to 3 weeks |
| Video interview integration | native interview operations from the platform | 1 to 2 weeks |
| SMS and OTP | stronger auth and notification coverage | 1 week |
| E-signatures | complete contract acceptance flow | 1 to 2 weeks |
| Saved searches and alerts | recruiter retention and repeated usage | 1 week |
| Advanced notification preferences | delivery routing across email, push, and in-app | 1 week |
| Mobile polish for production app stores | improved app-shell readiness and distribution hygiene | 1 to 2 weeks |

## Phase 3

Target:
- introduce deeper intelligence, forecasting, and marketplace services beyond the MVP hiring loop

### Candidate features

| Feature | Why it matters | Estimated effort |
|------|------|------|
| Custom ML ranking models | deeper matching quality and proprietary scoring | 4 to 6 weeks |
| Rich demand forecasting engine | forward-looking supply-demand planning | 3 to 5 weeks |
| Visa and relocation services | international placement support | 2 to 4 weeks |
| Multi-language product support | broader market reach | 2 to 3 weeks |
| Talent performance and placement intelligence | feedback-driven marketplace compounding | 2 to 4 weeks |
| Billing and contract automation suite | stronger marketplace operations | 3 to 5 weeks |

## Ordering rationale

- Phase 2 improves execution, monetization, and trust around the current product loop.
- Phase 3 adds differentiation and platform defensibility once the operational base is stable.

## What stays out until later

- a public job-board model
- broad ATS replacement workflows
- custom infra-heavy microservice expansion without a clear operating need
